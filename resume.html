Professional

After graduating high school, I went on to study Chemical Engineering at Kansas State University and graduated in 2009 at the height of the Great Recession. Through some combination of luck and merit, I had a job lined-up after graduation and went on to work at NCRA in McPherson, KS (now CHS Inc). This was my first professional job, and the people and company hold a special place in my heart.

After three years with NCRA, I took a job in Corpus Christ, TX with Flint Hills Resources. I was there for nearly 5 years and was afforded the opportunity to do a lot of really interesting things. Through my time at FHR, it became more obvious that I was destined to work more closely with software, and I left to chase an opportunity to model the U.S. Refineries at a small consultancy in Houston called Baker & O'Brien.

Fast forwarding a few years, we are still in Texas, but we moved from Corpus to Houston in 2017 for the job opportunity with Baker & O'Brien. I'm still modeling the U.S. Refineries as I was initially hired to do, but through a combination of automation and process improvements, I've managed to trim my initial position back to a part-time role. The total man-hours spent on software are roughly 15% of that from when I started.

Filling the gap I created through automation, I now do consulting in addition to being the only software developer on site.
